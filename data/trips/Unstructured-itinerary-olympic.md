Olympic Peninsula loop — 4 days, October 2 to 5, 2026. Two of us, driving the loop out of Seattle in the wagon.

Before we go: reserve the Lake Quinault Lodge for the last night (it books up fast), grab the ferry reservation for the Edmonds–Kingston crossing on the first morning, and pick up the National Parks pass. Budget we're aiming for is about $900 all in. Pack: rain shells (it's the Olympics), warm layers, hiking boots, the headlamps, a dry bag, and the good thermos.

Day 1 — Friday, Oct 2: Catch the 8:15am ferry from Edmonds over to Kingston. Drive up to Port Angeles and grab a quick lunch in town around noon. Afternoon up at Hurricane Ridge for the views — get there before the clouds roll in. Check in at the Olympic Lodge in Port Angeles for the night.

Day 2 — Saturday, Oct 3: Early start to Lake Crescent; kayak rental booked for 9:30. Picnic lunch by the water. Drive out to Sol Duc Falls in the afternoon, easy hike to the falls. Dinner back in Forks at 7, then overnight there.

Day 3 — Sunday, Oct 4: Out to the coast — Rialto Beach in the morning, walk to Hole-in-the-Wall at low tide (check the tide table the night before). Lunch from the cooler. Afternoon in the Hoh Rain Forest, the Hall of Mosses loop. Drive to Lake Quinault and check into the lodge.

Day 4 — Monday, Oct 5: Slow morning at the lodge, breakfast at 8. Quinault rain forest loop drive, stopping at the waterfalls. Lunch in Aberdeen on the way back, then the long drive into Seattle by evening. Fuel up before the return.
