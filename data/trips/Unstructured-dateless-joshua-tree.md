Joshua Tree long weekend — three days, no fixed dates yet, we'll lock them in once everyone's time off is confirmed. Two of us driving out from LA.

Before we go: reserve the Jumbo Rocks campsite (it books out fast) and double-check the park pass is still valid. Top off the tank before we leave cell service.

Packing: lots of water, sunscreen, the wide-brim hats, hiking boots, headlamps, a warm layer for the cold desert nights, and the star chart for stargazing.

Day 1: Leave LA early, breakfast on the road around 8am. Roll into the park through the West Entrance by late morning. Set up at Jumbo Rocks campground. Easy afternoon scramble around Skull Rock. Sunset at Keys View — get there by 6:30pm. Cook dinner at camp.

Day 2: Sunrise hike up Ryan Mountain, start at 6am while it's cool. Late breakfast back at camp. Midday drive down to Cholla Cactus Garden, then the Cottonwood Spring area. Dinner at Pappy & Harriet's in Pioneertown at 8pm — live music after, so don't be late. Back to camp for stargazing.

Day 3: Slow morning, pack up the campsite. Last walk through Hidden Valley around 10am. Lunch at Crossroads Cafe in the town of Joshua Tree. Drive back to LA in the afternoon.
