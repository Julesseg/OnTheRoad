Iceland ring road — a loose plan for a 5-day camper trip. We haven't locked the dates yet, so think of these as Day 1, Day 2, etc.

Before we go: book the camper (4x4, sleeps two), confirm the Blue Lagoon slot, and download the offline maps. Pack layers, swimsuits, a good rain shell, and the eye masks for the midnight sun.

Day 1: Land at Keflavík mid-morning, pick up the camper. Soak at the Blue Lagoon on the way out (pre-booked). Drive into Reykjavík, wander downtown, dinner at a harbour spot. Sleep at the campsite in Reykjavík.

Day 2: The Golden Circle. Þingvellir national park first thing, then the Geysir area around lunch, and Gullfoss waterfall in the afternoon. Push east and camp near Vík.

Day 3: South coast waterfalls — Seljalandsfoss (the one you can walk behind) and Skógafoss in the morning. Black sand beach at Reynisfjara, but watch the sneaker waves. Afternoon glacier walk on Sólheimajökull if the weather holds. Back to camp near Vík.

Day 4: Drive out to the Jökulsárlón glacier lagoon — long drive, leave early. Diamond Beach right across the road. Slow afternoon, maybe a short hike in Skaftafell. Camp in the Skaftafell area.

Day 5: Long drive back toward the airport. Stop at the Lava Centre if there's time. Return the camper and fly home in the evening. Don't forget to fuel up before dropping it off.
