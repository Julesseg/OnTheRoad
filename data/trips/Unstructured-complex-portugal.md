Portugal road trip — a week-ish in the spring, April 18 through April 24, 2026. Five of us in a rented van picking up in Lisbon and dropping in Porto.

Before we go: book the rental van (automatic, 7 seats) and confirm the Sintra palace tickets online — they sell out. Travel insurance still needs sorting. Rough budget is €2,400 all in, so ~€340/day; try to keep it there.

Packing: passports, the EU travel adapters, hiking shoes, swimsuits, a dry bag for the kayak day, sunscreen, and the good camera with the spare battery.

Saturday the 18th — land at Lisbon (LIS) around 11:15. Grab the van, drop bags at the Alfama guesthouse. Late lunch somewhere with a view, then just wander the Alfama. Fado dinner booked for 21:00 at Clube de Fado.

Day 2: Belém in the morning — the tower and the monastery, get there by 9 so it's not mobbed. Pastéis de Belém for the obvious reason, around noon. Drive out to Sintra in the afternoon; Pena Palace entry is timed for 3:30pm. Staying over in Sintra.

Mon Apr 20: slow start, breakfast at the inn. Quinta da Regaleira late morning. Then the long drive south to the Algarve — it's about 3 hours, so leave by 14:00. Check into the place in Lagos in the evening.

Tuesday: kayak tour of the Benagil sea caves, meet at the marina 9:45am sharp. Afternoon free on the beach. Seafood dinner at 8.

Apr 22 (Wed): drive north toward Óbidos, stop for lunch in the walled town (try the ginjinha). Wander the walls. Push on to Coimbra for the night — check into the hotel, nothing else planned.

Day 7-ish, the 23rd: morning at the old university and the Joanina Library (booked 10:30). Onward to Porto by mid-afternoon. Check in near Ribeira. Sunset drinks along the river.

Friday Apr 24 — last day. Port wine cellar tour across the river in Gaia at 11. Lunch, then drop the van at Porto airport by 16:00 for the evening flight home. Don't forget to fuel up before returning it.
